Debussy's music represents a departure from the traditional 19th-century musical language, challenging stereotyped harmonic procedures and orchestral conventions. He introduced innovative concepts like the "21-note scale" to disrupt tonality and transformed orchestral usage by treating each instrument almost as a soloist. Debussy also explored the piano's potential, embracing its unique capabilities. Later in life, he created an alter ego, Monsieur Croche, engaging in imaginary discussions about art and music.
